# Submission Assessment — v0.9.3 Pre-Human Evidence-Expanded

## What this package fixes

The prior v0.9.2 manuscript package contained only two headline tables. That was
insufficient for a reviewer to assess the breadth of individual metrics, the detailed
directional audit, the disposition of neutral evidence, or the role of Gemini 2.5
supporting studies.

v0.9.3 adds:

- main manuscript source and compiled PDF;
- a 15-page supplementary-results PDF;
- 19 detailed machine-readable result tables;
- an evidence inventory, validation/evidence disposition table, and a SHA-256
  provenance map for every copied aggregate table;
- an explicit neutral-evidence quarantine policy.

## What can be claimed

Primary Gemini 3.1 P1:
- Cross-evaluator replicated individual evidence for higher evaluator-assessed
  technical plausibility of M3 versus matched M1.
- Role-aware directional evidence for substantial creative-core preservation,
  practical MVP improvement, and overclaim removal.
- Directional prototype recommendation only as role-aware traceability evidence.

Supporting Gemini 2.5 P0/P1:
- Descriptive/developmental contextual evidence only.
- No raw-score pooling or generator-model ranking.

No claim:
- neutral global preference/equivalence/superiority;
- human preference;
- factual correctness;
- market viability, adoption, revenue, or startup success.

## Remaining pre-submission risks

1. Human evaluation is not yet collected, so external human validation cannot be claimed.
2. The paper should be moved into the target journal's official template and its
   reference style before submission.
3. The manuscript needs a final duplicate-card audit/sensitivity analysis if the
   42 Gemini duplicate flags remain unresolved.
4. The repository must remain private or reviewer-controlled until human study
   blinding is no longer vulnerable.

## Desk-screening impact

Adding the full paper evidence and supplementary tables removes a transparency
weakness. It does not remove the core limitation that all reported outcomes are
LLM-evaluator assessments. The package is now appropriate for supervisor review or
controlled reviewer evidence; it is not a final public v1.0 human-validated release.
